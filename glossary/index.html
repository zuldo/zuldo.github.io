Glossary

I may edit glossary entries at any time, without indicating it.

Mental delta

A.K.A. "cogstrat" for "cognitive strategy".

The difference between a thought and the next.

Examples:

  • See person -> retrieve their name from memory.
  • Notice that an improvement would take less than 10 seconds to implement -> implement it.
  • Experience felt sense -> do Focusing on it.

(These examples aren't very good, because neither is my skill at noticing these (yet).)

(Concept stolen from Be Well Tuned's Tune Your Cognitive Strategies.)

Compare with TAPs.

Mental delta reinforcement

Rewarding/reinforcing a mental delta, a la Tune Your Cognitive Strategies.

Empathy

Hardware, or virtualization of such hardware, in human brains, that specialize in modelling other humans. Often extends partially to nonhumans as well. Probably also occurs in some other species. Often has certain mental patterns leaking through from the model to the human using it, especially emotions. Probably emulation, not simulation.

If mirror neurons exist in humans empathy uses them.

Compassion is a pattern of usage of the output of empathy.

Compassion

A pattern of usage of the output of empathy. See here.

Goodharting

Replacing a goal with a proxy for the original goal, while still believing you're optimizing for the original goal.

Color scheme

Literal: a set of colors used in a graphic.

Metaphorical: a set of cached decisions used in an information generator, that can be swapped with other such sets with the same structure.

Positive metafeedback

The heuristic [When receiving feedback from a person, reward them for giving you feedback, and for their feedback being accurate and sincere.]. This is separate from reaction to the object-level feedback content.

Ifwhen

"X ifwhen Y" = "If Y happens, then [when Y happens, X]". Like "when" but without certainty about it ever applying.

Self-pushing/forcing

Going against parts of yourself when...

  1. those parts do not serve your goals.
  2. those parts do serve your goals. This usually happens when there's internal conflict regarding the goals and actions in question, and/or when something external is controlling you.

Creative/receptive activity

An activity where information flows...

  1. outwards from you.
  2. inwards to you.

Vernacular

A color scheme for communication through language-primary mediums, such as text.

Queerodivergent

[Logical OR]/[set-theoretical union] of [queer] and [neurodivergent].

Absolute/relative [unit of time/space]

A section of time/space that...

  1. snaps/rounds to set points (e.g. "calendar day" or "clock hour").
  2. is relative to a frame of reference (e.g. "for 2 hours" or "since I woke up").
