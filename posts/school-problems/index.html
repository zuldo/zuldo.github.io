School problems

Here I will list problems with school and attempt to process related trauma. Based partially on direct observation, partially on indirect observation, partially on extrapolation.

  • This list is not exhaustive. And what I've picked up on is biased towards mostly containing information related to the reference classes I'm in.
  • The categories are not exclusive, I put subitems under whichever parents I think they fit the best under.
  • I will update this list over time.

See also:

  • The True Face of the Enemy
  • I Am Conscious and I Am Hurting

Body autonomy and discomfort

  • Bathrooms.
    • Usage restricted by "teachers".
      • Encourages dehydration.
    • Tiny room size.
    • Unusable sinks.
      • Cold water.
      • Not enough space.
  • Forced physical presence.
  • Arbitrary restrictions on body usage.
    • Stimming/fidgeting.
    • Looking direction.
      • "Look at the person speaking.".
      • "Keep your eyes forward.".
    • Clothing limitations.
      • Keeping hoodie hoods down.
  • Uncomfortable chairs.
    • And as far as I know forced chair usage. I never actually tried standing up in school for the purpose of avoiding discomfort.

Emotions and health

  • Surpression of pain.
    • Proportial, sane responses to school-induced pains are disincentivized.
    • "Everyone goes through this.".
    • "There are people who have it worse.".
    • "You just have to push through the pain.".
  • Breaking mental systems for feelings about self.
    • Pushing "students" into unstable, dysfunctional identities.
      • Self-worth based on grades.
  • Food.
    • Many schools don't even feed their "students" to begin with.
      • Selling food is not feeding.
    • Withholding or not displaying information about ingredients.
      • See also "Against autistic people.". Eating uncomfortable ingredients feels even worse when they're a surprise.
  • Tiring schedules.

Freedom

  • Discouragement of "free time".
    • Homework with no upper limit of possible effort to put into it.

Social

  • Disencouragement of respecting children.
    • It's painful to both respect someone and accept them being subjected to large amounts of pain.
  • Forced participation in social webs and surpressing some social emotions.
    • "Everybody gets to join.".
    • "We're all friends here.".
    • "Why do you not like [person]?".

Prejudical discrimination

  • General/several.
    • Not counteracting hostility.
      • Lots of teen cishet people literally use "gay" as an insult. There are lots of teen cishet people in schools.
    • Spreading misinformation and withholding important information.
      • About sex and gender.
      • About attraction.
      • About available medical care.
        • Gender-related treatments such as HRT.
        • How to get medical care in general.
  • Against autistic people.
    • [See "Arbitrary restrictions on body usage.". Actually, see most of "Body autonomy and discomfort".]
    • Bright lights.
    • Loud noises.
    • Unreliable schedules.
    • In some cases active hostility.
      • "Autistic people lack empathy.".
      • "People tend to avoid eye contact when lying.".
  • Against women.
    • Clothing limitations determined by gender or sex (not inherently against women specifically, but I've only observed it against women in practice).
  • Against queer people.
    • Forced usage of changing rooms segregated by AGAB or sex.
    • Forced clothing.
      • All types of distinguishing between different people which I have observed school uniforms being designed and enforced for are:
        • School year.
        • AGAB or sex (possibly gender, but I doubt it).
    • Bathrooms segregated by AGAB or sex.

Anti-epistemics and anti-rationality

  • Encourages or causes certain biases.
    • "Guessing the Teacher's Password".
  • Anti-epistemics to protect lies and hide problems.
    • Ignoring obviously counterproductive schedules.
    • Using anti-epistemics is directly "taught" in the form of "debates", by encouraging ability to make untrue things sound true without separation between that fake "belief" used for lying/reality-ignoring, and actual beliefs.
      • Encourages assuming and practicing communication adversarially.
