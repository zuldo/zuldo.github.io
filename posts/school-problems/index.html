School problems

Here I will list problems with "school" and attempt to process related trauma. Based partially on direct observation, partially on indirect observation, partially on extrapolation.

  • This list is not exhaustive. And what I've picked up on is biased towards mostly containing information related to the reference classes I'm in.
  • The categories are not exclusive, I put subitems under whichever parents I think they fit the best under.
  • I might update this list over time.

I quote "school", "student", and "teacher" because those words are lies when describing what I'm talking about here.

This post is currently mostly speaking to children and teenagers stuck in "school". As in, when I write "you" that's the people I'm referring to.

See also:

Body autonomy and discomfort

  • Bathrooms.
    • Usage restricted by "teachers".
      • Encourages dehydration.
    • Tiny room size.
    • Unusable sinks.
      • Cold water.
      • Not enough space.
  • Forced physical presence.
  • Arbitrary restrictions on body usage.
    • Stimming/fidgeting.
    • Looking direction.
      • "Look at the person speaking.".
      • "Keep your eyes forward.".
    • Clothing limitations.
      • Keeping hoodie hoods down.
  • Uncomfortable chairs.
    • And as far as I know forced chair usage. I never actually tried standing up in "school" for the purpose of avoiding discomfort.

Emotions and health

  • Gaslighting.
    • See most of this post.
    • Everything adults say about "schools" that doesn't admit that those are inherently hostile eventually turns into lying to pretend like they're not.
      • Your parents are very probably complicit in this.
    • Here's some anti-gaslighting:
      • You're allowed to feel emotions, including pain and anger.
      • You deserve freedom.
      • Other people controlling your body is a violation of freedom. Punishing you for not keeping it in specific states counts as controlling it.
      • You're allowed to get hurt by things that other people don't seem to get hurt by.
      • If you want more help/support/advice, feel free to contact me.
  • Surpression of pain.
    • Proportial, sane responses to "school"-induced pains are disincentivized.
    • "Everyone goes through this.".
    • "There are people who have it worse.".
    • "You just have to push through the pain.".
  • Breaking mental systems for feelings about self.
    • Pushing "students" into unstable, dysfunctional identities.
      • Self-worth based on grades.
  • Food.
    • Many "schools" don't even feed their "students" to begin with.
      • Selling food is not feeding.
    • Withholding or not displaying information about ingredients.
      • See also "Against autistic people.". Eating uncomfortable ingredients feels even worse when they're a surprise.
    • Sometimes "students" are forced to choose between risking malnutrition and eating dead bodies.
  • Tiring schedules.

Freedom

  • The inherent violation of freedom that is forcing people to be at locations, and the one that is forcing them do certain things.
  • Discouragement of "free time".
    • Homework with no upper limit of possible effort to put into it.

Social

  • Disencouragement of respecting children towards adults.
    • It's painful to both respect someone and accept them being subjected to large amounts of pain. Most adults choose the latter.
  • Forced participation in social webs and surpressing some social emotions.
    • "Everybody gets to join.".
    • "We're all friends here.".
    • "Why do you not like [person]?".
