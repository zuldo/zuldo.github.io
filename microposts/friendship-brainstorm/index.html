friendship-brainstorm

Exercise: Set a 5 minute timer, and list as many concrete ideas as you can for experiments to run, and things to try doing differently.

- Intentionally Making Close Friends

Here is the output of my 5 minutes.

  • ask people what i do that makes them happy
  • ask people what sort of mistakes i tend to do
  • try different things with different people, or at different times
  • people are in different moods at different times, account for this
  • friendships are not competitions
  • making your friends happy is fun
  • learn about love languages
  • respond well to criticism
  • practice giving criticism in respectful, constructive ways
  • tell people when they're hurting you
  • don't demand things from people
  • relationships should be freeing, not limiting
  • reaching out to people is a better idea than it feels like
  • generally, you're more averse to many social things than you should be
  • gradually try more difficult things so you get less averse to things that will lead to better relationships
  • actually care about people
  • encourage people's relationships with people who are not you
  • take inspiration from how other people's relationships work
  • imitate people that make you happy
  • leave ways out of everything, no obligations
  • small acts of affection add up over time, and are easier than sparse large ones
  • ask people what they're comfortable with
  • be curious about people and the things they talk about
  • encourage weirdness and passion
  • practice some things with people you trust a lot before trying them in unfamiliar contexts
