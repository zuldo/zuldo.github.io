What I've been learning and thinking about recently

  • Efficient time usage for most people is probably what looks from a naive perspective like inefficient time usage.
    • Hobbies.
    • In general things that exist to take up time such as board games.
  • Socializing is often driven by inherently social, or by no specific, goals.
    • I learned this from observing other people having conversations that were so obviously not about things such as arriving at conclusions it overpowered my biases for assuming such things.
    • Also, ironically, from observing socialization in fiction.
      • People really do just do things because they're fun!
        • The reason I took so long to really get this is probably related to dissociation and such.
  • Not being genuine is difficult.
    • And very rarely worth it.

    • If you once tell a lie, the truth is ever after your enemy.

  • Specific situations makes not hurting people difficult even if you don't actually want them to hurt.
    • And some arguments/debates/fights are positive feedback loops of things other than truth-seeking and policy-designing.
      • Are communication algorithms that aren't anger-like much more complicated than anger-like ones, and so those tend to get used by default?
      • Or is it literally just about sticking to teams?
      • I should read more about psychology about politics and "politics".
    • It gets easier with practice, though, as you recieve more evidence about how to do it well.
    • I wish I was able to teach the parts of this that I have.
  • Boundaries are there because you want to keep a relationship existing.
    • Without them, either the relationship collapses or you push away the other person.
  • Epistemic humility is difficult.
    • Especially about things that are instrumentally important, even though that's nonsensical.
    • Especially when talking with other people.
  • The ways in which I construct and name concepts are unusual.
    • "valid"
    • "lazy"
    • "preference"
  • Many debates/arguments are redundant, exist in social reality but not reality.
    • You already know your relevant preferences well enough to make acceptable approximations of optimal tradeoffs.
      • Example: respecting people's chosen pronouns. Figuring out how to act regarding chosen pronouns takes like five minutes. You only need to talk to other people about it to know what ratios of [not hurting people] versus [not caring about people sufficiently different from oneself] are common around you, and how people react to them. The rest you can extrapolate (assuming you already know what pronouns and preferences and pain are).
        • Like there really are not discussions about whether to respect people's chosen pronouns. The "whether" implies making decisions as opposed to [whatever is going on, as mentioned before I have not yet studied "politics"].
        • If you believe I made any object-level argument about pronoun usage here you misinterpreted this.

Self-deception is bad.

There's a "productivitiy trick" that goes something like, "Tell yourself that you will only do a minute of [procrastinated activity]. This makes it easier to keep going, so you can keep [doing the activity] for more than a minute."

In other words "Lie to 'your'self about how long you indent to [do something], to get 'your'self to give you control that you then use to break that self-promise."

There's a reason you're not doing the thing in question. Admit to yourself what "your" reasons are instead of fighting "your"self and you can do what all of you actually want, whether or not that's the original activity.

Do not attempt to "trick yourself". Cooperate.

Note about a certain failure mode.

  1. You declare something about yourself that you endorse (or think you do).
    • Examples:
      • "I'm a kind person."
      • "I don't actually care about other people."
    • Probably occurs mostly with things that feel morality-related.
  2. There are parts of you that don't follow the pattern.
    • This is a consequence of declaring instead of observing.
  3. Those parts are forced out of the places in your mind where the "you" doing the endorsement are.
    • Such as becoming subconscious because the endorsement is conscious.
    • See also this.
  4. Less conscious control, "akrasia", internal conflict, self-hate.

I don't know how to avoid this in general. But stuff in the general cluster pointed to by "self-acceptance"/"self-love" seems to help. Also in some cases possibly [redacted].
